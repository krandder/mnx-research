ADL Stress Findings

The independent ADL chain is separate from the volume replay. It executes one hypothesis at a time, records a focused regression, and lets the previous result choose the next risk surface.

"Local fix" means a code change and regression test committed on the local exchange-replay branch. It does not mean the exchange branch was pushed, merged, deployed, or released.

  • A14: Receipt identity mismatch

    Bug found; local fix

    Receipt validation accepted a payload when the round and submission existed independently, even if the submission belonged to another round or market. The fix binds receipt round, submission, and market identity before journaling.

  • A15: Submitted tx hash mismatch

    Bug found; local fix

    A receipt with the correct round, submission, and market could still use a different tx hash. The fix rejects receipts whose submitted tx hash provenance does not match.

  • A16: Reconciliation-failed stale recovery

    Bug found; local fix

    Stale recovery could abandon a round after RECEIPT_RECONCILIATION_FAILED, reopening the market while chain effects were still unknown. The fix keeps that frozen repair state pinned.

  • A17: Duplicate ADL trade log index

    Bug found; local fix

    A successful receipt could include two ADL trade updates with the same log index, deriving duplicate ADL settlement identities. The fix rejects duplicate log indices before publishing a receipt fact.

  • A18: Zero-effect successful receipt

    Bug found; local fix

    A decoded successful receipt with no ADL trades and no position or settlement effects could release the active ADL round. The fix quarantines it as a reconciliation failure and keeps cancel-only pinned.

  • A19: Post-ADL reduce-only close capacity

    Active

    The active hypothesis tests whether a reduce-only close order that was valid before ADL can become stale after an ADL position update and still seal after ADL-owned cancel-only releases.
